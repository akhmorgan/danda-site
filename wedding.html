Travel & Transportation

How to get to the venue.

Via the magic of Maps

The Winter Gardens, Hexham

Or for the less technically inclined...

By Car - A69 from Newcastle
  • Turn Left at the Bridge End Roundabout
  • Go over the river and straight over the next roundabout
  • Turn left at the mini roundabout, and follow the road round to the right
  • Turn right at the mini roundabout
  • Straight on through the main shopping street, over 5 pedestrian traffic lights
  • At the 6th traffic lights turn left onto Alston/Allendale Road (B6305)
  • Take the third right and follow the driveway to the top
By Car - A69 from Carlisle
  • Turn right at the B6531, following signs to Hexham
  • Carry on straight to the traffic lights (about 2 miles)
  • Take a sharp right up to Alston/Allendale Road (B6305)
  • Take the third right and follow the driveway to the top
By Train - From Newcastle or Carlisle

It is about 1 mile (25 minute walk) from the station to the venue.

  • Travel to Hexham Station.
  • Turn left outside the station.
  • Follow the same route as a car coming off of the A69 from Newcastle.

Our Story

And so the adventure began...

  • October 2010

    Our Humble Beginnings

    The exact date on which this happened, has been a matter of much debate between us. Neither of us can remember, so we agreed upon a ceremonial date for anniversary purposes.

    The date itself was a lovely italian meal. Followed by a nice impromptu walk on the quayside and a trip to the Baltic. During which we bought some hand puppet animal sticker things. Some of which we still have lying around somewhere! A word to the wise: don't order linguine on a first date. There is no attractive way to eat that.

  • May 2011

    Our first vacation

    Sunshine, swimming, and perilous mountain dune buggying. A great holiday; and that buggy was awesome. Cheaper than a car and only a fraction of the functionality. It only had 2 gears: forwards and reverse... and reverse was a bit hit and miss!

  • November 2014

    The popping of a certain question

    All dressed up for a wonderful dinner at a fancy restaurant, with champagne on ice. Well, that was the plan anyway... until a certain someone decided that they didn't want to get all dressed up or go anywhere fancy.

    So a new plan was formed.

    A location was scouted by sprinting down to the quayside from work, every lunchtime for a week.

    A romantic walk along the river was planned for the warmest day we were going to get for a while.

    The ring was snuck into a shoulder bag. Said bag was then fiddled with, much to the grooms dismay.

    After a lovely walk and some coercion the bride was in place. The groom surreptitiously got the ring out of the bag and was about to take a knee....

    Then the bride decided a photo needed to be taken, so she stepped backwards into the groom, almost knocking the ring out of his hand! He then panicked and awkwardly hid the ring behind his back. After some more coercion, amidst complaints of "but I haven't got he whole scene in the photo yet!", the bride was once again in place

    TL;DR - She said "Yes".

  • July 2015

    Our first house

    A wedding on a budget clearly wasn't stressful enough for us... so we set about buying a house!

  • December 2015

    We can has kitten!

    We've wanted a pet for a good long while. So when the chance to adopt an adorable little kitten came our way, we jumped at the chance. So now we have an adorable little fur ball in our lives.

F.A.Q.

The answers to a few frequently asked questions about the day

Gifts?

Your presence at our wedding is all that we wish for. However, if you want to give a gift, we will be grateful for a small cash donation towards our honeymoon and planning the rest of our future together.

Can I bring a date?

Unfortunately, no.
Unless we have explicitly stated otherwise on your invitation.

We have worked really hard to create a small, intimate celebration featuring all of our most important people.

Thanks for understanding.

Will there be an open bar?

Table drinks and fizz for toasting will be provided. After that there will be a CASH ONLY bar. Card payments won't be accepted, so please bring some cash to avoid disappointment.

If you have any further questions about any of these points or anything else about the wedding, please don't hesitate to contact us via email
